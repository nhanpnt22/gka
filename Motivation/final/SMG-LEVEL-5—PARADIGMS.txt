1. PARADIGMATIC ROLE

This level defines the emergence and stabilization of recurring regulatory configurations into persistent system regimes.

Function:
- Aggregate Level 4 regulatory dynamics into stable configurations
- Encode long-run structure of behavior, constraint interaction, and outcomes

Output:
- A set of stable paradigms Π
- Each paradigm is a statistically invariant regime over system trajectories

Constraint:
- No action-level optimization (Level 8)
- No domain specialization (Level 7)
- No abstraction beyond observed regularities (Level 6)

--------------------------------------------------

2. INPUT FROM META-THEORY

Given Level 4 system:

ℛ_SMG =
(λ_S(t), λ_M(t), τ_S(t), f_S, f_M, h_S, α_t, β_t)

Define observable trajectory:

Ξ_t := (λ_S(t), λ_M(t), τ_S(t), r_t, d_t, g_t)

Where:
- r_t := R_S(a_t)
- d_t := D_M(a_t)
- g_t := G(a_t)

--------------------------------------------------

3. PARADIGM DEFINITION

Let ℙ denote the space of probability measures over the trajectory space.

Let d(·,·) be a metric on ℙ (e.g., Wasserstein or total variation).

Define an equivalence relation ~ over trajectories:

Ξ ~ Ξ'  iff

lim_{t→∞} d( Law(Ξ_t), Law(Ξ'_t) ) = 0

A paradigm Π is an equivalence class:

Π := [Ξ] under ~

Thus, paradigms are defined as asymptotically distributionally equivalent trajectory classes.

--------------------------------------------------

4. PARADIGM REPRESENTATION

Each paradigm Π is represented by a limiting distribution μ_Π ∈ ℙ over:

(λ_S, λ_M, τ_S, r, d, g)

Define:

Π = (μ_Π)

Equivalently summarized by:

Π = (Λ_S, Λ_M, Τ_S, Σ_R, Σ_D, Σ_G)

Where:

- Λ_S := support of λ_S under μ_Π
- Λ_M := support of λ_M under μ_Π
- Τ_S := support of τ_S under μ_Π
- Σ_R, Σ_D, Σ_G := marginal distributions of r, d, g

--------------------------------------------------

5. STABILIZATION CRITERIA

A paradigm Π is stable if:

(S1) Distributional Stationarity:
For all k ≥ 0:
lim_{t→∞} d( Law(Ξ_t), Law(Ξ_{t+k}) ) = 0

(S2) Parameter Tightness:
λ_S(t), λ_M(t), τ_S(t) remain in compact subsets with high probability

(S3) Regulatory Closure:
E[f_S(Δ_S)] = 0
E[f_M(Δ_M)] = 0

(S4) Viability Preservation:
P(Ξ_t ∈ 𝒱) → 1

--------------------------------------------------

6. PARADIGM CLASSES

(P1) Survival-Dominant:
λ_S >> λ_M

(P2) Meaning-Dominant:
λ_M >> λ_S

(P3) Gain-Dominant:
λ_S, λ_M → λ_min

(P4) Balanced:
λ_S, λ_M both bounded away from λ_min and λ_max

--------------------------------------------------

7. PARADIGM TRANSITIONS

Define stochastic transition operator:

Τ: 𝒫_SMG → Δ(𝒫_SMG)

Where:
- Δ(𝒫_SMG) is the space of probability measures over paradigms

Thus:

Τ(Π_i) = distribution over next paradigms

Transitions are induced by:

- Persistent constraint violations
- External perturbations
- Drift in regulatory functions

--------------------------------------------------

8. PATH DEPENDENCE

The induced paradigm process is non-Markovian:

P(Π_{t+1} | Π_t, history) ≠ P(Π_{t+1} | Π_t)

Sources:
- accumulated belief structures (Level 2)
- identity inertia (M_t dynamics)
- parameter hysteresis (λ dynamics)

--------------------------------------------------

9. MULTI-SCALE STRUCTURE

Paradigms exist at multiple scales:

- Individual
- Group
- Institutional
- Civilizational

Scaling effect:
Local paradigm stability may induce global coordination or conflict.

--------------------------------------------------

10. PARADIGM METRICS

Define:

Φ(Π) :=
( E[g_t], −E[r_t], −E[d_t] ) under μ_Π

This induces a Pareto partial order over paradigms.

--------------------------------------------------

11. FAILURE OF PARADIGMS

A paradigm Π collapses if:

(F1) Viability Breakdown:
P(Ξ_t ∉ 𝒱) does not converge to 0

(F2) Parameter Divergence:
λ_S(t), λ_M(t) fail to remain tight

(F3) Signal Instability:
E[r_t] or E[d_t] diverges or remains persistently high

--------------------------------------------------

12. PARADIGMATIC CLOSURE

Define:

𝒫_SMG := set of all stable paradigms Π

Assume:
- 𝒫_SMG is non-empty
- 𝒫_SMG is closed under the topology induced by d on ℙ
