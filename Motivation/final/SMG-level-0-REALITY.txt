SURVIVAL–MEANING–GAIN (SMG) SYSTEM
LEVEL 0 — GENERATIVE REALITY (PROCESS)

Author: Phạm Ngọc Thanh Nhân (Brian Pham)
Date: March 24, 2026

--------------------------------------------------
1. GENERATIVE ROLE

This level defines the pre-ontological generative process from which all SMG structures emerge.

Function:
- Generates conditions of existence
- Produces the possibility of structure, value, and constraint

Output:
- The necessary preconditions for Level 1 (Ontology)

Property:
- Pre-epistemic
- Pre-structural
- Non-representational

No objects, sets, mappings, or formal relations are defined at this level.

--------------------------------------------------
2. GENERATIVE PROCESS

Reality is defined as a continuous generative process:

𝒢

such that:

𝒢 ⇒ emergence of distinctions

These distinctions are not predefined entities, but arise through differentiation internal to the process.

There is no fixed state space, action space, boundary, or separable structure—only ongoing generation.

--------------------------------------------------
3. EMERGENCE OF DISTINCTION

The primary outcome of 𝒢 is distinguishability:

𝒢 ⇒ (difference)

From difference, the following proto-conditions become possible:

- separation (proto-state)
- variation (proto-action)
- comparability (proto-value)

These are not formal objects, but preconditions for their later definition.

--------------------------------------------------
4. PRECONDITIONS FOR ONTOLOGY

Level 0 generates the minimal conditions required for Level 1:

- Persistent distinctions enable the formation of a state space S
- Transformable variation enables the formation of an action space A
- Comparable outcomes enable the formation of a value space V

Identity (M) emerges from stabilized patterns of distinction.
Constraint structures (e.g., Survival, Meaning) emerge from persistent regularities.

None of these are defined at this level; only their possibility is generated.

--------------------------------------------------
5. CONTINUITY AND OPEN-ENDEDNESS

The generative process 𝒢 is:

- continuous (not discretely indexed)
- non-terminating
- non-teleological
- unconstrained

There is no objective, selection principle, or evaluation mechanism at this level.

--------------------------------------------------
6. PRE-AXIOLOGICAL CONDITION

No value exists at Level 0.

There is:
- no preference
- no utility
- no ordering over outcomes

Value (Gain) becomes definable only after structure (Level 1) and representation (Level 2).

--------------------------------------------------
7. PRE-CONSTRAINT CONDITION

No constraints exist at Level 0.

There is:
- no survival requirement
- no identity restriction
- no feasibility boundary

Constraint parameters (e.g., λ_S, λ_M, τ_S) arise only after structural formation.

--------------------------------------------------
8. PRE-CAUSAL CONDITION

No formalized causal structure exists at this level.

There is:
- no state transition system
- no temporal indexing
- no deterministic or stochastic law

Causality becomes definable only after the introduction of structured states and relations.

--------------------------------------------------
9. REPRESENTATIONAL INDEPENDENCE

The generative process 𝒢 is not defined in terms of any representation.

All higher-level constructs (S, A, V, M, Ω, etc.) are:

- derived from 𝒢
- not intrinsic to 𝒢

Thus, structure is emergent rather than fundamental.

--------------------------------------------------
10. TRANSITION TO LEVEL 1 (ONTOLOGY)

Level 1 begins when distinctions satisfy stabilization conditions:

- persistence: distinctions endure across generation
- repeatability: distinctions can be re-identified
- separability: distinctions can be treated as discrete elements

Formally:

𝒢 ⇒ stabilization ⇒ (S, A, V, M, Ω, ...)

This marks the transition from generative process to structured ontology.

--------------------------------------------------
11. INTERPRETATION

Level 0 defines reality as pure generative potential.

- It contains no Survival, Meaning, or Gain
- It enables their emergence as structural constraints at higher levels

Thus:

SMG is not fundamental at Level 0,
but arises as a structured system beginning at Level 1.

--------------------------------------------------
END — LEVEL 0 (GENERATIVE REALITY)
