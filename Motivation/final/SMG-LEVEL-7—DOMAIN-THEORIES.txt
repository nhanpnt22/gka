- Enable empirical modeling and falsifiability

3. DOMAIN THEORY DEFINITION

A domain theory D ∈ 𝒟 is defined as:

D :=
(S_d, A_d, Ω_d, V_d, M_d, P_d, C_d, R_d, D_d, Θ_d)

Where:

State Space:
S_d ≠ ∅

Action Space:
A_d ≠ ∅

Feasibility:
Ω_d : S_d ⇉ A_d, Ω_d(s) ≠ ∅

Value Space:
V_d (normed vector space)

Identity Constraint:
M_d ⊆ V_d, closed, non-empty

Dynamics / Belief:
P_d defined as either:
- Transition kernel: P_d(· | s, a), or
- Belief distribution: P_d(s)

Cost:
C_d : A_d → ℝ₊

Survival (Risk):
R_d : S_d × A_d → [0,1]

Meaning (Deviation):
D_d : V_d → ℝ₊

Parameters:
Θ_d = (λ_S, λ_M, τ_S)

--------------------------------------------------

4. DOMAIN VALUE CONSTRUCTION

Raw value:
V_d : S_d × A_d → V_d

Meaning projection:
Proj_{M_d}(v) ∈ argmin_{u ∈ M_d} ||u − v||

Projected value:
V_M^d(a) =
E_{s ~ P_d}[ Proj_{M_d}(V_d(s, a)) ]

Objective function (axiological instantiation):

J_d(a) =
E_{s ~ P_d}[ Proj_{M_d}(V_d(s,a)) ]
− C_d(a)
− λ_S · E_{s ~ P_d}[ R_d(s,a) ]
− λ_M · D_d( E_{s ~ P_d}[Proj_{M_d}(V_d(s,a))] )

Note:
J_d is a domain-instantiated realization of the Level 3 axiological functional J.

--------------------------------------------------

5. DOMAIN CONSISTENCY CONDITIONS

A valid domain theory must satisfy:

(D1) Measurability of all mappings
(D2) Non-empty feasibility: Ω_d(s) ≠ ∅ ∀ s
(D3) Projection well-defined (existence of minimizer)
(D4) Finite objective: J_d(a) < ∞
(D5) Structural invariance with Level 6

6. DOMAIN SPECIALIZATION TYPES

Domains instantiate SMG by assigning meanings to S, M, G:

(1) Artificial Intelligence
- S: system stability, constraint satisfaction
- M: alignment, objective correctness
- G: reward / performance
Insight: Optimization without alignment ⇒ unsafe behavior

(2) Economic Systems
- S: solvency, risk exposure
- M: legal/institutional constraints
- G: profit, growth
Insight: Markets optimize efficiency, not externalities

(3) Cognitive Systems
- S: physical/mental stability
- M: identity, beliefs, coherence
- G: reward, satisfaction
Insight: Identity constrains perceived value

(4) Social / Political Systems
- S: stability, regime survival
- M: ideology, legitimacy
- G: power, influence
Insight: Cohesion ↑ ⇒ conflict ↑

(5) Biological Systems
- S: survival, homeostasis
- M: genetic constraints
- G: reproduction, energy efficiency
Insight: Evolution optimizes reproduction, not well-being
