--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 13.1 — INFORMATION-THEORETIC FORMULATION
Dual of Evolutionary Selection (Canonical)
--------------------------------------------------

0. PURPOSE

Provide an information-theoretic dual to Level 13,
showing that evolutionary selection is equivalent
to entropy-regularized concentration under survival constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

1. WORLD

A world is a constrained stochastic dynamical system:

ω = (X, ℬ(X), Φ, ℛ)

Where:

X        := measurable state space
ℬ(X)     := σ-algebra on X
Φ        := Markov kernel Φ: X × ℬ(X) → [0,1]
ℛ ⊂ X    := non-empty measurable feasible region

Let μ_t ∈ Δ(X) denote a population distribution.

--------------------------------------------------

2. SURVIVAL FUNCTIONAL

Let A ⊂ ℛ be a persistent set.

Define:

F(x) := P_x( X_t ∈ A ∀ t ≥ 0 )

Assume:

∫_X F(x) μ_t(dx) > 0

Define:

f(x) := log F(x), with F(x) = 0 ⇒ f(x) = -∞

--------------------------------------------------

3. ENTROPY

Let λ be a σ-finite reference measure on (X, ℬ(X)).

Assume:

μ ≪ λ

Define:

H(μ) := - ∫_X log(dμ/dλ) dμ

Thus H is finite and well-defined.

--------------------------------------------------

4. FREE ENERGY

For τ > 0, define:

ℱ(μ) := - ∫_X f(x) μ(dx) + τ H(μ)

Equivalently:

ℱ(μ) = -E_μ[log F(x)] + τ H(μ)

--------------------------------------------------

5. VARIATIONAL CHARACTERIZATION

Consider:

sup_{μ ∈ Δ(X), μ ≪ λ} [ E_μ[f(x)] - τ H(μ) ]

Assume:

∫_X exp(f(x)/τ) dλ(x) < ∞

Then a maximizer μ* exists and satisfies:

dμ*/dλ (x) = exp(f(x)/τ) / Z

where:

Z := ∫_X exp(f(x)/τ) dλ(x)

Hence:

μ*(x) ∝ F(x)^{1/τ}

--------------------------------------------------

6. STRUCTURE

- μ* concentrates on high-survival states
- τ controls exploration–concentration trade-off

Limits:

τ → 0  ⇒  support(μ*) ⊂ argmax_x F(x)
τ > 0  ⇒  soft concentration over near-optimal states

--------------------------------------------------

7. DYNAMICS

Define:

μ_{t+1}(A) =
∫_X F(x) Φ(x, A) μ_t(dx)
--------------------------------
∫_X F(x) μ_t(dx)

Equivalently:

μ_{t+1} ∝ F ⋅ (Φ_* μ_t)

--------------------------------------------------

8. VARIATIONAL INTERPRETATION

Let ν_t := Φ_* μ_t.

Then μ_{t+1} satisfies:

μ_{t+1} ∈ argmin_{μ ≪ λ} [
D(μ || ν_t) - E_μ[f(x)]
]

where:

D(μ || ν) := ∫ log(dμ/dν) dμ

Thus:

evolution = KL projection under exponential tilting.

--------------------------------------------------

9. ENTROPY BALANCE

- Selection (F) concentrates mass
- Propagation (Φ) disperses mass

Entropy evolution reflects this competition;
it is not monotone in general.

--------------------------------------------------

10. FIXED POINTS

A stationary μ* satisfies:

μ*(A) =
∫_X F(x) Φ(x, A) μ*(dx)
--------------------------------
∫_X F(x) μ*(dx)

Such μ* concentrate on high-survival regions.

--------------------------------------------------

11. EQUIVALENCE WITH LEVEL 13

Level 13:

selection induces preorder via F

Level 13.1:

selection induces variational structure via ℱ

Thus:

ordering by survival ⇔ entropy-regularized optimization of log-survival.

--------------------------------------------------

12. INTERPRETATION

Evolution corresponds to:

reweighting probability mass toward higher-survival states
subject to entropy constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

13. CORE LAW (INFORMATION FORM)

Evolution is governed by:

ℱ(μ) = -E_μ[log F(x)] + τ H(μ)

with dynamics implementing entropy-regularized
concentration of survival probability.

--------------------------------------------------

14. CONCLUSION

Selection is equivalent to
entropy-regularized inference over survival.

Therefore:

evolution = constrained information processing
in stochastic dynamical systems.
