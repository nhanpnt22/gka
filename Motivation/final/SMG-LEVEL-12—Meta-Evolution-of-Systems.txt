Level 12 — Meta-Evolution of Systems (Civilizational Selection Dynamics)

1. Space of Systems

Let 𝒮 denote the set of all admissible Level 11 systems:
    S = (ℛ_SMG^i, G, μ, R, 𝒰, Ψ, 𝒞)

Assume:
- 𝒮 is a Polish space (complete, separable metric space)
- Equipped with Borel σ-algebra ℬ(𝒮)

Let μ_t ∈ Δ(𝒮) denote a probability measure over systems.

---

2. System-Level Performance Functional

Define:
    W(S) = E [ Σ_{t=0}^∞ γ^t J(ℐ_t^S) ]

where:
- γ ∈ (0,1)
- ℐ_t^S is the aggregate state under system S
- J: ℐ → ℝ is measurable

Assumptions:
- J is bounded, i.e. |J(ℐ)| ≤ C
  OR
- J is integrable such that W(S) is finite ∀ S ∈ 𝒮

Thus W(S) is well-defined.

---

3. Collapse Event (Stopping Time)

Let ℐ_safe ⊂ ℐ be a closed measurable subset (safe region).

Define collapse time:
    τ_collapse(S) := inf { t ≥ 0 : ℐ_t^S ∉ ℐ_safe }

Then:
- τ_collapse is a stopping time w.r.t. filtration generated by ℐ_t^S

Define survival probability:
    F(S) := P(τ_collapse(S) = ∞)

---

4. Mutation Kernel

Let 𝒰 be a Markov kernel:
    𝒰: 𝒮 × ℬ(𝒮) → [0,1]

such that:
- For each S ∈ 𝒮, 𝒰(S, ·) is a probability measure
- For each measurable A ⊂ 𝒮, 𝒰(·, A) is measurable

---

5. Replicator–Mutation Dynamics

Population evolution:
    μ_{t+1}(A) =
        ∫_𝒮 F(S) 𝒰(S, A) μ_t(dS)
        --------------------------------
        ∫_𝒮 F(S') μ_t(dS')

for all measurable A ⊂ 𝒮

This defines a mapping:
    Ψ_meta: Δ(𝒮) → Δ(𝒮)

---

6. Equilibrium Definition

A stationary distribution μ* satisfies:
    μ* = Ψ_meta(μ*)

Assume:
- Δ(𝒮) equipped with weak-* topology
- Ψ_meta is continuous under this topology

Then fixed points exist (by Schauder / Kakutani-type arguments).

---

7. Stability Characterization

If:
- F(S) is strictly concave over a convex subset of Δ(𝒮)
  OR
- F admits a unique maximizer S*

Then:
- μ* concentrates on argmax_S F(S)
- i.e., μ* → δ_{S*}

Otherwise:
- Multiple attractors may exist
- System exhibits path dependence and regime coexistence

---

8. Timescale Separation

Assume hierarchy:
- Fast: Level 3 (utility optimization)
- Medium: Level 4 (constraint adaptation)
- Slow: Level 10–11 (institutional evolution)
- Slowest: Level 12 (system selection)

Thus:
    ℐ_t^S ≈ stationary distribution under fixed S

and:
    F(S) depends on long-run system behavior

---

9. Interpretation

Level 12 defines:

- Variation → via mutation kernel 𝒰
- Selection → via survival probability F(S)
- Retention → via replication in μ_t

Thus:

    Civilization dynamics =
    stochastic evolutionary process over institutional systems

---

10. Full System Closure

Level 12 closes the hierarchy:

Level 3 → value
Level 4 → regulation
Level 5–9 → structure & paradigms
Level 10 → multi-agent interaction
Level 11 → institutional systems
Level 12 → evolutionary selection over systems

---

Final Object:

𝓔_meta = (𝒮, μ_t, F, 𝒰, Ψ_meta)

This defines a well-posed stochastic evolutionary dynamical system over civilizations.
