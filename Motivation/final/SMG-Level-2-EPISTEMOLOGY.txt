1. EPISTEMIC ROLE

This level defines the representation of ontological structure.

Function:
- Construct representations of Level 1 elements and relations
- Encode uncertainty over states, mappings, and identity

Output:
- Structured epistemic objects (beliefs, models, representations)

Constraint:
- No optimization, evaluation, or action selection
- No value-based comparison (axiology begins at Level 3)
- No adaptive or time-evolving learning dynamics

--------------------------------------------------

2. EPISTEMIC PRIMITIVES

2.1 Observation Space

Let:
𝒪 be a non-empty set of observations

--------------------------------------------------

2.2 Information Space

Let:
ℐ denote the set of admissible information states (histories), where:

ℐ ⊆ ⋃_{t≥0} (𝒪 × A)^t × 𝒪

Each element ℐ_t ∈ ℐ is a finite sequence of observations and actions.

ℐ ≠ ∅

--------------------------------------------------

2.3 Belief Space

Let:
𝒫(S) denote the set of probability measures over S

Elements:
b ∈ 𝒫(S)

--------------------------------------------------

2.4 Representation Space

Let:
ℳ_rep denote a class of admissible representations such that:

- Each element is a measurable function or correspondence
- Domains and codomains are consistent with Level 1 structures

ℳ_rep contains representations of:
Ω, V_raw, Risk_S, Deviation_M

--------------------------------------------------

3. BELIEF REPRESENTATION

Define the belief mapping:

b : ℐ → 𝒫(S)

such that:

b(ℐ_t) = P(S_t | ℐ_t)

Interpretation:
- b encodes uncertainty over the true ontological state
- Beliefs depend on accumulated information histories

No assumption of correctness, optimality, or convergence is imposed.

--------------------------------------------------

4. REPRESENTATION OF STRUCTURAL MAPPINGS

The system admits epistemic representations of Level 1 mappings:

- Feasibility representation:
  Ω̂ : S ⇉ A

- Value representation:
  V̂_raw : S × A → V

- Risk representation:
  R̂isk_S : S × A → ℝ_{≥0}

- Deviation representation:
  D̂eviation_M : V → ℝ_{≥0}

Each of the above belongs to ℳ_rep.

These are internal representations of ontological mappings:
(Ω, V_raw, Risk_S, Deviation_M)

No requirement of accuracy, consistency, or convergence is imposed.

--------------------------------------------------

5. IDENTITY REPRESENTATION

Define an epistemic identity representation:

M̂ ⊆ V

such that:
- M̂ ≠ ∅ (optional but typical)
- M̂ represents a perceived or inferred identity structure
- M̂ may differ from the ontological identity M

Projection representation:

Proĵ_M : V ⇉ M̂

such that:

Proĵ_M(v) ∈ argmin_{u ∈ M̂} ||u − v||

This defines epistemic access to identity constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

6. KNOWLEDGE STRUCTURE

An epistemic configuration is defined as:

K = (b, Ω̂, V̂_raw, R̂isk_S, D̂eviation_M, M̂)

where:

- b ∈ 𝒫(S)
- Ω̂, V̂_raw, R̂isk_S, D̂eviation_M ∈ ℳ_rep
- M̂ ⊆ V

K is a representation of the ontological system, not the system itself.

No component of K is assumed to be derivable from another at this level.

--------------------------------------------------

7. UNCERTAINTY STRUCTURE

Uncertainty is intrinsic and multi-layered:

- State uncertainty:
  represented by b ∈ 𝒫(S)

- Structural uncertainty:
  discrepancy between (Ω̂, V̂_raw, …) and true mappings

- Identity uncertainty:
  discrepancy between M̂ and M

Uncertainty is represented but not resolved at this level.

--------------------------------------------------

8. EPISTEMIC RELATIONS

The epistemic layer is characterized by the following mappings:

Information → Belief:
ℐ_t ↦ b(ℐ_t)

Value representation:
(s, a) ↦ V̂_raw(s, a)

Risk representation:
(s, a) ↦ R̂isk_S(s, a)

Deviation representation:
v ↦ D̂eviation_M(v)

Identity projection:
v ↦ Proĵ_M(v)

These relations describe how knowledge represents structure,
without implying transformation, learning, or control.

--------------------------------------------------

9. EPISTEMIC CONSTRAINTS

At Level 2, the following are excluded:

- Objective functions or utility
- Optimization or maximization
- Action selection or policies
- Constraint enforcement
- Adaptive learning or parameter updates
- Normative or evaluative judgments

All constructs are descriptive and representational only.

--------------------------------------------------

10. EPISTEMIC CLOSURE

The SMG epistemic system is fully defined by:

𝒦_SMG =
(𝒪, ℐ, 𝒫(S), ℳ_rep, b, Ω̂, V̂_raw, R̂isk_S, D̂eviation_M, M̂)

This specifies:

- observation structure
- information structure
- belief representation
- structural representations
- identity representation
- uncertainty encoding

No additional epistemic primitives are required.

--------------------------------------------------

11. INTERPRETATION

Level 2 defines knowledge as structured representation of ontology:

- States are not directly accessed, but inferred via beliefs
- Structural mappings are not given, but represented
- Identity is not fixed, but epistemically constructed

Thus:

Level 1 (Ontology) defines what exists  
Level 2 (Epistemology) defines what is known about what exists  

No decision, value judgment, or behavior is implied.
