STATE SPACE AND STRUCTURE

Let the augmented state be:
    X_t = (S_t, M_t, λ_S(t), λ_M(t))

Where:
- (S, 𝔅(S)) is a Borel (Polish) state space
- (A, 𝔅(A)) is a Borel action space
- Ω: S → 2^A is a measurable correspondence with non-empty, compact values
- V is a separable Banach space with norm ||·||_V
- M_t ⊂ V is a non-empty, closed, bounded subset
- The metric on subsets M_t is induced by the Hausdorff distance d_H

Projection well-posedness:
Assume either:
    (i) V is a Hilbert space, or
    (ii) M_t is non-empty, closed, convex, and proximinal
and the projection multifunction:
    (M, v) ↦ argmin_{u ∈ M} ||u − v||_V
has a measurable graph, ensuring existence of a jointly measurable selector.

Constraint weights:
    λ_S(t), λ_M(t) ∈ [λ_min, λ_max] ⊂ (0, ∞)

Define the survival-feasible correspondence:
    Ω_S(X_t) = { a ∈ Ω(S_t) | Risk_S(X_t, a) ≤ τ_S(t) }

--------------------------------------------------
MODEL DEFINITIONS

(1) Augmented Transition Kernel:
    P_X(· | X_t, a)

A stochastic kernel on the augmented state space X governing:
    (S_{t+1}, M_{t+1}, λ_S(t+1), λ_M(t+1))

Assume:
    P_X is measurable in X_t and weakly continuous in a.

(2) Projection Operator:
    Proj_{M_t}(v) ∈ argmin_{u ∈ M_t} ||u − v||_V
(selected measurably)

(3) Meaning-Projected Value:
    V_M(X_t, a) =
        E[ Proj_{M_t}( V_raw(S_{t+1}, a) ) | X_t, a ]

(4) Objective Function:
    J(X_t, a) =
        V_M(X_t, a)
        − C(X_t, a)
        − λ_S(t) · Risk_S(X_t, a)
        − λ_M(t) · Deviation_M(X_t, a)

(5) Bellman Operator (on bounded measurable functions V ∈ B_b(X)):
    (TV)(X_t) =
        sup_{a ∈ Ω_S(X_t)} [
            J(X_t, a)
            + γ · E_{X_{t+1} ~ P_X}[ V(X_{t+1}) ]
        ]

(6) Optimal Action-Value:
    Q*(X_t, a) =
        J(X_t, a)
        + γ · E[ V*(X_{t+1}) | X_t, a ]

--------------------------------------------------
ASSUMPTIONS

(A1) Measurability & Selection  
Ω(S_t) is measurable with compact values.  
Ω_S(X_t) is measurable and admits a universally measurable selector.

(A2) Joint Measurability  
All primitives:
    V_raw, Risk_S, Deviation_M, C, Proj_{M_t}
are jointly measurable in (X_t, a).

(A3) Regularity  
J(X_t, a) is continuous in a and bounded.

(A4) Constraint Regularity  
Risk_S(X_t, a) is lower semicontinuous in a,
ensuring Ω_S(X_t) is compact.

(A5) Transition Kernel  
P_X(· | X_t, a) is a well-defined stochastic kernel,
measurable in X_t and weakly continuous in a.

(A6) Discounting  
γ ∈ (0,1)

(A7) Two-Timescale Stochastic Approximation  

Fast timescale (policy/value):
    step sizes η_t:
        Σ η_t = ∞,   Σ η_t² < ∞

Slow timescale (constraints):
    λ_S(t+1) = λ_S(t) + α_t f_S(X_t, a_t) + ξ_t^S
    λ_M(t+1) = λ_M(t) + α_t f_M(X_t, a_t) + ξ_t^M

    Σ α_t = ∞,   Σ α_t² < ∞  
    α_t / η_t → 0  

    f_S, f_M Lipschitz  
    ξ_t are bounded martingale difference sequences

(A8) Identity Dynamics  
M_{t+1} = g(M_t, S_{t+1}) with:
    d_H(M_{t+1}, M_t) ≤ δ

(A9) Exploration (Optional, Feasible)  
β_t ≥ 0, β_t → 0  
Σ β_t = ∞, Σ β_t² < ∞  
Exploration policy is GLIE and respects Ω_S(X_t)

(A10) Convexity (for Pareto result)  
The attainable set:
    { (Gain, Risk_S, Deviation_M)(X_t, a) : a ∈ Ω_S(X_t) }
is convex.

(A11) Lyapunov Condition (Stability)  
There exists L: X → ℝ₊ such that:
    E[L(X_{t+1}) | X_t] − L(X_t) ≤ −c L(X_t) + d

(A12) Viability (for multi-step feasibility, optional)  
Ω_S is invariant under P_X:
    a ∈ Ω_S(X_t) ⇒ P_X(Ω_S(X_{t+1}) ≠ ∅) = 1

--------------------------------------------------
RESULTS

(1) Existence of Optimal Policy  

There exists a universally measurable Markov policy π* such that:
    π*(X_t) ∈ argmax_{a ∈ Ω_S(X_t)} Q*(X_t, a)

--------------------------------------------------

(2) Bellman Contraction & Uniqueness  

T is a γ-contraction on (B_b(X), ||·||∞):
    ||T V1 − T V2||∞ ≤ γ ||V1 − V2||∞

Thus:
- A unique fixed point V* exists  
- Value iteration converges globally to V*

--------------------------------------------------

(3) One-Step Feasibility Preservation  

For any admissible policy π:
    π(X_t) ∈ Ω_S(X_t) ⇒
    P(Survival_{t+1} | X_t, π(X_t)) ≥ 1 − τ_S(t)

Multi-step feasibility holds under (A12).

--------------------------------------------------

(4) Two-Timescale Convergence  

Under (A7), the coupled process:
    (π_t, λ_S(t), λ_M(t))
converges almost surely to an internally chain transitive (ICT) invariant set
of the associated limiting ODE.

If the ODE admits a globally asymptotically stable equilibrium:
    (π*, λ_S*, λ_M*)
then convergence to this equilibrium holds.

--------------------------------------------------

(5) Pareto Efficiency (Qualified)  

Under (A10), J is a valid scalarization of:
    (Gain, −Risk_S, −Deviation_M)

Thus π* is Pareto-efficient over Ω_S(X_t):
no feasible action strictly improves one objective without worsening another.

--------------------------------------------------

(6) Stochastic Stability  

Under (A11):
- sup_t E[L(X_t)] < ∞  
- V*(X_t) is bounded  

If additional irreducibility conditions hold:
- {X_t} is positive recurrent

--------------------------------------------------

(7) Exploration & Optimality  

Under (A9):
- All feasible actions are explored infinitely often (w.p.1)  
- Convergence to π* is preserved  

--------------------------------------------------

(8) Instability / Collapse Conditions  

If any holds:

(i) λ_S(t) → 0 and ∃ ε > 0 such that  
    inf_{a ∈ Ω(S_t)} Risk_S(X_t, a) ≥ ε  

(ii) λ_M(t) → 0 and ∃ ε > 0 such that  
    inf_{a ∈ Ω(S_t)} Deviation_M(X_t, a) ≥ ε  

(iii) Ω_S(X_t) = ∅ infinitely often with non-zero probability  

Then the system becomes unstable:
- survival failure  
- identity incoherence  
- policy degeneracy or divergence  

--------------------------------------------------

CONCLUSION

The SMG-OM defines a well-posed constrained stochastic control system over an augmented state space in which:
- Survival enforces feasibility constraints  
- Meaning enforces identity-consistent admissibility  
- Gain defines the optimization objective  

Under standard regularity, stochastic approximation, and (optional) convexity and Lyapunov conditions, the system admits:
- a unique optimal value function  
- a universally measurable optimal policy  
- convergence under adaptive constraints  
- Pareto-efficient behavior  

Motivation is thus formally characterized as the emergent optimal policy of a survival-constrained, identity-projected stochastic control process.
