--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 14A — MECHANISM / INSTITUTION DESIGN
Equilibrium Shaping under Constraints (Canonical)
--------------------------------------------------

0. PURPOSE

Extend Level 13.3 by introducing mechanisms that
modify incentives, information, and constraints
to shape equilibrium outcomes.

Establish that:

institutions are admissible transformations that
induce desired equilibria within feasibility limits.

--------------------------------------------------

1. BASE SYSTEM

A multi-agent system:

ω = (X, ℬ(X), {A_i}_{i=1}^N, Φ, ℛ, {r_i})

Let:

Π_eq(ω) := set of (soft) Nash equilibria

--------------------------------------------------

2. MECHANISM CLASS

Let 𝓜 be a set of admissible mechanisms.

Each mechanism:

M ∈ 𝓜 : ω ↦ ω'

where:

ω' = (X, ℬ(X), {A_i'}, Φ', ℛ', {r_i'})

subject to feasibility constraints:

- limited control over rewards, dynamics, or information
- enforceability constraints
- observability constraints
- resource / budget constraints

--------------------------------------------------

3. OBJECTIVES

Let O be a desired outcome set:

O ⊂ X   or   O ⊂ Δ(X)

Let J be a system-level objective functional:

J: trajectories or distributions → ℝ

Design goal:

Find M ∈ 𝓜 such that induced equilibria optimize J
and/or concentrate probability mass on O.

--------------------------------------------------

4. IMPLEMENTATION CONCEPTS

Let Π_eq(ω') be equilibria under M.

Weak implementation:

∃ π* ∈ Π_eq(ω') such that:
P^{π*}(outcome ∈ O) > 0

Full implementation:

∀ π* ∈ Π_eq(ω'):
P^{π*}(outcome ∈ O) = 1

Robust implementation:

implementation persists under perturbations of ω' and agents.

--------------------------------------------------

5. INCENTIVE COMPATIBILITY

A mechanism M is incentive-compatible if:

∀ i, ∀ π* ∈ Π_eq(ω'):

π_i* ∈ argmax_{π_i} V_i^{ω'}(π_i, π_{-i}*)

Thus:

desired behavior must be individually optimal.

--------------------------------------------------

6. ENTROPY-REGULARIZED FORM

Under entropy regularization:

π_i*(a_i|x) ∝ exp(
Q_i^{ω'}(x,a_i,·) / τ
)

Thus:

mechanism design operates by shaping Q_i^{ω'}.

--------------------------------------------------

7. DESIGN LEVERS

Mechanisms act through four minimal channels:

(1) PAYOFF SHAPING
    r_i → r_i'
    (taxes, subsidies, penalties)

(2) INFORMATION STRUCTURE
    modify signals / observability
    affects beliefs and expectations

(3) ACTION CONSTRAINTS
    A_i → A_i'
    restrict feasible strategies

(4) DYNAMICS / ENVIRONMENT
    Φ → Φ', ℛ → ℛ'
    alter transition structure or feasibility

--------------------------------------------------

8. POTENTIAL / ALIGNMENT STRUCTURE

In structured settings (e.g., potential or aligned systems):

∃ Φ_potential such that:

equilibrium behavior corresponds to optimization
of a shared objective.

In such cases:

P^{π} ∝ exp(
Σ_{t=0}^∞ γ^t Φ_potential(X_t,a_t) / τ
)

Otherwise:

no global objective representation is required.

--------------------------------------------------

9. PERFORMANCE LOSS (PRICE OF ANARCHY)

Let J be a system-level objective.

Define:

PoA :=
(supremum of J over feasible trajectories) /
(infimum of J over equilibrium-induced trajectories)

Mechanism design aims to:

reduce PoA by aligning incentives with J.

--------------------------------------------------

10. VIABILITY-CONSTRAINED DESIGN

Let:

𝒱 := { x : ∃ π such that
P_x^{π}(X_t ∈ ℛ ∀ t ≥ 0) > 0 }

Admissible mechanisms must satisfy:

equilibria π* preserve viability:

P_x^{π*}(X_t ∈ ℛ ∀ t ≥ 0) > 0

Thus:

design operates within survival constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

11. DYNAMICAL IMPLEMENTATION

Let adaptation dynamics:

π_{t+1} = 𝒯(π_t)

A mechanism is dynamically implementable if:

π_t → Π_eq(ω') and induced behavior achieves O
or optimizes J.

Thus:

design must consider convergence, not only equilibria.

--------------------------------------------------

12. META-SELECTION OVER MECHANISMS

Let 𝓜 be the space of admissible mechanisms.

Define:

𝓜* := { M ∈ 𝓜 : M yields desirable equilibria }

Under system evolution:

- mechanisms compete
- stable mechanisms persist

Thus:

selection operates over institutions.

--------------------------------------------------

13. UNIFIED VIEW

- Level 13: selection over states
- Level 13.1: selection over distributions
- Level 13.2: control over trajectories
- Level 13.3: equilibrium over strategies
- Level 14A: design over equilibria

Thus:

adaptive systems can be shaped by modifying
their structural rules.

--------------------------------------------------

14. CORE LAW (DESIGN FORM)

An optimal mechanism M ∈ 𝓜 satisfies:

Π_eq(ω') ⊂ argmax_{π} J(P^{π})

subject to:

- incentive compatibility
- viability constraints
- feasibility of M

and induces:

π_i*(a_i|x) ∝ exp( Q_i^{ω'} / τ )

--------------------------------------------------

15. CONCLUSION

Institutions are constrained transformations of
multi-agent systems that reshape incentives,
information, and feasibility to select equilibria.

Therefore:

design = constrained control over interaction

and:

evolutionary systems are not only adaptive,
but governable through mechanism design.
