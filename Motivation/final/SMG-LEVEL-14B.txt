--------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 14B — LEARNING / EVOLUTIONARY DYNAMICS
Convergence to Equilibrium in Adaptive Systems (Canonical)
--------------------------------------------------

0. PURPOSE

Extend Levels 13–14A by introducing dynamics that govern
how agents and systems evolve over time.

Establish that:

learning dynamics are the processes through which
selection, control, interaction, and design are realized.

--------------------------------------------------

1. BASE SYSTEM

Given a designed multi-agent system:

ω' = (X, ℬ(X), {A_i}, Φ, ℛ, {r_i})

Let:

Π_eq(ω') := set of (soft) Nash equilibria

--------------------------------------------------

2. POLICY SPACE AND DYNAMICS

Let Π be the space of admissible policy profiles.

Let 𝓣 be a class of admissible learning rules.

A learning dynamic:

𝒯 ∈ 𝓣,   𝒯: Π → Π

induces:

π_{t+1} = 𝒯(π_t, observations_t)

subject to informational and computational constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

3. STOCHASTIC EVOLUTION

Learning induces a joint stochastic process over:

- states: X_t
- policies: π_t

Evolution:

X_{t+1} ~ Φ^{π_t}(X_t)
π_{t+1} = 𝒯(π_t, observations_t)

Thus:

state and policy co-evolve.

--------------------------------------------------

4. FIXED POINTS

A policy π* is stationary if:

𝒯(π*) = π*

If additionally:

π* ∈ Π_eq(ω')

then π* is an equilibrium fixed point.

--------------------------------------------------

5. CLASSES OF LEARNING DYNAMICS

Canonical classes include:

(1) BEST-RESPONSE DYNAMICS
    π_{t+1} ≈ argmax V_i(·, π_{-i,t})

(2) GRADIENT / POLICY GRADIENT
    π_{t+1} = π_t + η ∇ V_i(π_t)

(3) REPLICATOR DYNAMICS
    growth proportional to relative payoff

(4) BAYESIAN UPDATES
    posterior-based policy adaptation

(5) NO-REGRET DYNAMICS
    time-averaged regret → 0

--------------------------------------------------

6. ENTROPY-REGULARIZED DYNAMICS

In entropy-regularized settings:

π_{t+1}(a|x) ∝ exp( Q^{π_t}(x,a) / τ )

Thus:

learning corresponds to iterative exponential tilting.

This form does not apply universally,
only under entropy-regularized objectives.

--------------------------------------------------

7. CONVERGENCE

A learning dynamic converges if:

π_t → π* as t → ∞

Types:

- pointwise convergence
- convergence in distribution
- time-average convergence

Convergence is not guaranteed in general,
and depends on:

- payoff structure (e.g., potential games)
- contraction properties
- noise and exploration

--------------------------------------------------

8. STABILITY

An equilibrium π* is:

- Lyapunov stable if nearby trajectories remain close
- asymptotically stable if trajectories converge to π*
- stochastically stable if robust under noise

Stability depends on:

- learning rule 𝒯
- system structure
- perturbations

--------------------------------------------------

9. SELECTION VIA DYNAMICS

Learning induces selection:

- unstable equilibria are not observed
- stable equilibria persist

Thus:

Π_eq(ω') is refined to dynamically stable subsets.

--------------------------------------------------

10. NO-REGRET AND EQUILIBRIA

Under no-regret dynamics:

time-averaged play converges to:

- coarse correlated equilibrium (general case)
- Nash equilibrium (in specific settings)

Thus:

different dynamics select different solution concepts.

--------------------------------------------------

11. PATH DEPENDENCE

Outcomes may depend on:

- initial condition π_0
- stochastic fluctuations
- exploration processes

Thus:

multiple equilibria imply history-dependent outcomes.

--------------------------------------------------

12. TIMESCALE AND ADAPTATION

Let η denote learning rate.

Trade-off:

- high η → rapid adaptation, possible instability
- low η → stability, slower convergence

Thus:

timescale affects both convergence and equilibrium selection.

--------------------------------------------------

13. META-DYNAMICS

Let 𝓣 be the set of admissible learning rules.

Define:

𝓣* := { 𝒯 ∈ 𝓣 : induces desirable outcomes }

subject to:

- informational constraints
- computational feasibility
- implementability

Under evolution:

- learning rules compete
- stable/adaptive dynamics persist

Thus:

selection operates over dynamics.

--------------------------------------------------

14. UNIFIED VIEW

- Level 13: selection (existence)
- Level 13.1: distributions (variational structure)
- Level 13.2: control (agents)
- Level 13.3: equilibrium (interaction)
- Level 14A: design (institutions)
- Level 14B: learning (dynamics)

Thus:

adaptive systems evolve through iterative processes
toward structured equilibria under constraints.

--------------------------------------------------

15. CORE LAW (DYNAMIC FORM)

Adaptive systems evolve via:

π_{t+1} = 𝒯(π_t)

and, when convergence holds:

π_t → π* ∈ Π_eq(ω')

subject to:

- learning dynamics 𝒯 ∈ 𝓣
- mechanism constraints M ∈ 𝓜
- viability constraints ℛ

In entropy-regularized cases:

𝒯 approximates exponential tilting of Q-functions.

--------------------------------------------------

16. CONCLUSION

Learning dynamics are the temporal realization of:

- selection (Level 13)
- control (Level 13.2)
- interaction (Level 13.3)
- design (Level 14A)

Therefore:

adaptive systems evolve through constrained,
iterative dynamics toward stable equilibria.
