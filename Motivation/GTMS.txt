GENERAL THEORY OF MOTIVATED SYSTEMS 
A Minimal, Complete, and Falsifiable Theory of Behavior and Emergence

--------------------------------------------------

1. CORE AXIOM

All non-random behavior arises from agents selecting actions to maximize expected value over perceived possibilities, under resource constraints and uncertainty.

--------------------------------------------------

2. MINIMAL CANONICAL FORM

a_t ~ π(a | P_t, Ω_t, V_t)

π(a) ∝ exp(β · Q(P_t, a))

Q(P_t, a) = E_{s ~ P_t} [ Σ (γ^k · r_{t+k}) ]

Core Identity:
Behavior = f(P, Ω, V)

Where:
P = Perception (belief over states)
Ω = Possibility (action space)
V = Value (preferences)

Note:
All expectations are taken over the agent’s belief distribution unless otherwise specified.

--------------------------------------------------

3. ENVIRONMENT (DYNAMICS)

s_{t+1} ~ T(s_t, a_t, a_{-i})

- Environment evolves dynamically
- Includes multi-agent interaction

--------------------------------------------------

4. PERCEPTION (BOUNDED INFERENCE)

P_t ≈ P(s_t | I_t)

P_t = argmin [ Inference Error + λ · Information Cost ]

- Perception is a probabilistic belief over reality
- Limited by attention, bandwidth, and computation

--------------------------------------------------

5. VALUE (STRUCTURE OF MOTIVATION)

V_t = V_identity + V_adaptive

r_t = f(s_t, internal_t, social_t)

Components:
- Biological (survival, reward)
- Internal (goals, identity)
- Social (status, incentives)

--------------------------------------------------

6. CONSTRAINTS (ACTION SPACE)

Ω_t ⊆ A(K_t, R_t)

- Limited by knowledge, resources, and rules

--------------------------------------------------

7. RESOURCE-BOUNDED POLICY

π ∈ Constraints(memory, attention, compute)

- Agents optimize under cognitive and computational limits

--------------------------------------------------

8. LEARNING DYNAMICS

V_{t+1} = V_t + α (r_t - r̂_t)
P_{t+1} = Update(P_t, I_t)
Ω_{t+1} = Expand(K_t) - Constraints

--------------------------------------------------

9. MULTI-AGENT INTERACTION

a_i ~ π_i(a_i | P_i, Ω_i, V_i)

- Agents act probabilistically
- Environment co-evolves through interaction

--------------------------------------------------

10. CONTROL THEORY

Control ⇔ E[|Δ(P, V, Ω)_external|] > E[|Δ(P, V, Ω)_self|]

Control channels:
- Perception (information shaping)
- Value (incentive shaping)
- Possibility (constraint shaping)

--------------------------------------------------

11. EMERGENCE (MICRO → MACRO)

M_t = Φ({P_i, Ω_i, V_i})

M_{t+1} = F(M_t, {a_i})

Where:
Φ = aggregation function
F = macro-dynamics function

- Macro systems emerge from agent interaction
- Includes markets, cultures, and institutions

--------------------------------------------------

12. EQUILIBRIUM & ATTRACTORS

Equilibrium:
a_i* = argmax V_i(a_i, a_{-i}*)

Attractors:
State → Stable(P, V, Ω)

Types:
- Stable
- Metastable
- Unstable

--------------------------------------------------

13. FAILURE MODES

- P ≠ s (misperception)
- argmax V ≠ optimal (value misalignment)
- |Ω| ↓ (constraint lock-in)
- V_identity → external control (identity corruption)
- Information cost > capacity (cognitive overload)

--------------------------------------------------

14. FALSIFIABILITY

GTMS is invalid if:

- Changing P does not change behavior
- Changing V does not change decisions
- Changing Ω does not affect behavior diversity

--------------------------------------------------

15. AUTONOMY PRINCIPLE

Autonomy = Control(P) + Control(V_identity) + Expansion(Ω) - Overload

--------------------------------------------------

16. SCOPE CONDITIONS

Applies to:
- Humans
- AI agents
- Organizations
- Societies

Does NOT apply to:
- Purely random systems
- Systems without value optimization
- Systems without perception-action loops

--------------------------------------------------

17. FINAL MASTER FORM

P(a_t) ∝ exp [ β · E_{s ~ P_t} ( Σ γ^k · f(s_t, internal_t, social_t) ) ]

Subject to:
- s_{t+1} ~ T(s_t, a_t, a_{-i})
- P_t minimizes inference + information cost
- a_t ∈ Ω_t
- Policy constrained by resource limits

--------------------------------------------------

FINAL STATEMENT

Behavior and macro-level systems emerge from resource-bounded agents who probabilistically optimize identity-constrained value over belief-weighted possibilities, where perception defines reality, constraints define action, and interaction produces stable and unstable structures.
