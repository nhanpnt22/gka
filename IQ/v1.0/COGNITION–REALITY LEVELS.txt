COGNITION–REALITY LEVELS (ALIGNED — SURVIVAL-FIRST, INTERNALLY CONSISTENT)

--------------------------------------------------

0. GENERATIVE REALITY (GROUND TRUTH PROCESS)

Function:
- generates true states, outcomes, and constraints
- defines the unknown causal process linking actions to consequences

Structure:
(S_{t+1}, Y_t, C_{t+1}) ~ P_real,t(· | S_t, a_t, C_t)
O_t ~ g_t(· | S_t, Y_{t-k}, a_{t-k})

Properties:
- partial observability: O_t ≠ S_t (noisy, delayed, manipulable)
- non-stationarity: P_real,t evolves with hidden regime shifts
- endogeneity: a_t → S_{t+k}, C_{t+k}
- adversarial dynamics: strategic agents distort signals
- heavy-tailed & path-dependent: rare events dominate; irreversibility constrains transitions
- constraint evolution: C_t changes and may bind nonlinearly

Implications:
- true state, constraints, and risk are systematically underestimated
- observations are not ground truth
- model validity is temporary
- feedback delay creates risk of acting before learning

Output:
- latent state S_t, outcomes Y_t, observations O_t, constraints C_t

--------------------------------------------------

1. ONTOLOGY (STATE CONSTRUCTION)

Function:
- constructs decision-relevant state from imperfect observations

State:
Ŝ_t = {x_t, ẑ_t, ĉ_t, h_t}

x_t: observed variables
ẑ_t: latent proxies (biased, uncertain)
ĉ_t: inferred constraints (risk, liquidity, irreversibility, delay, resources)
h_t: compressed history (path dependence, regime context)

Construction:
Ŝ_t = f(O_{1:t}, a_{1:t-1})

Principles:
- approximate sufficiency: P(Y | Ŝ_t, a) ≈ P(Y | history, a)
- minimality: dim(Ŝ_t) ↓ subject to sufficiency
- defensive completeness: include latent risk + constraint proxies
- validity: holds only while predictive performance is stable

Properties:
- partial observability (Ŝ_t ≠ S_t)
- constraint misestimation (ĉ_t ≠ C_t)
- state aliasing and compression loss
- endogeneity: a_t → Ŝ_{t+k}

Rule:
If Ŝ_t is incomplete or unstable → reduce exposure (not increase complexity)

Output:
- decision state Ŝ_t, ĉ_t with uncertainty

--------------------------------------------------

2. EPISTEMOLOGY (MODEL SYSTEM)

Function:
- produces probabilistic predictions under uncertainty and instability

Model Set:
M = {m_i}, weights w_i,t

P(Y | Ŝ_t, a) = Σ w_i,t · P_i(Y | Ŝ_t, a)

Update:
w_i,t+1 ∝ w_i,t · exp(−loss_i,t), Σ w_i,t = 1

Constraints:
- no single-model dominance
- enforced diversity
- correlated failure risk (N_eff,t < nominal)

Uncertainty:
U_t = U_epistemic + U_aleatoric
U_t ≥ U_min > 0

Calibration:
E[predicted] ≈ E[realized]

Confidence:
c_t = f(U_t, ε_cal,t, D_t, N_eff,t)

Implications:
- models are incomplete and temporary
- uncertainty and risk are structurally underestimated

Adaptive Constraint:
adaptation speed ≥ instability × exposure
else → reduce exposure

Output:
- P(Y | Ŝ_t, a), U_t, {w_i,t}, diagnostics

--------------------------------------------------

3. VALUE & OBJECTIVE (HIERARCHICAL)

Function:
- defines survival-constrained optimization

Hard Constraints:

1. Survival:
P(irreversible loss | Ŝ_t, ĉ_t, a) < δ

2. Constraint Respect:
P(non-recoverable violation | Ŝ_t, ĉ_t, a) < ε_c

Feasible Set:
A_safe = {a satisfying constraints}

--------------------------------------------------

Within A_safe:

- maximize optionality Ω(a)
- maximize learning IG(a)
- maximize value E[Y | Ŝ_t, a] − λ·Risk(a)

Ranking:
J_eff(a) = c_t · E[Y | Ŝ_t, a] − (1 − c_t) · DownsideRisk(a)

Rule:
lower objectives cannot violate higher constraints

Output:
- A_safe, ranked actions

--------------------------------------------------

4. DECISION & CONTROL (POLICY)

Function:
- selects actions under uncertainty and constraints

Policy:
π(Ŝ_t) → a_t ∈ A_safe

Modes:

PROTECT:
- high uncertainty / instability / constraint proximity
→ minimize exposure

EXPLORE:
- disagreement or error rising
→ small, diversified, information-seeking actions

EXPLOIT:
- stable regime, high confidence
→ controlled optimization

--------------------------------------------------

Survival Gate:

Reject or scale actions if:
- irreversible loss risk
- delay risk (Δt_feedback > Δt_recovery)
- hidden / latent exposure
- correlation / systemic risk
- constraint violation
- destabilizing endogeneity

--------------------------------------------------

Sizing:

size(a) ∝ c_t × evidence × stability

Constraints:
- exposure ≤ risk budget
- d(exposure)/dt ≤ learning rate
- U_t ↑ → size ↓

Output:
- action a_t, size(a_t), exposure profile

--------------------------------------------------

5. EXECUTION & INTERACTION

Function:
- maps decisions into real outcomes under P_real,t

(S_{t+1}, Y_t, C_{t+1}) ~ P_real,t

Properties:
- unknown, non-stationary
- action-dependent
- adversarial and feedback-driven

Risks:
- slippage, delay, hidden constraints
- model–reality mismatch
- endogenous instability

Output:
- realized state transition, outcome, constraint updates

--------------------------------------------------

6. FEEDBACK & ADAPTATION

Function:
- updates system under delayed, distorted feedback

-------------------------

6.1 Observation

Õ_t = W(O_{1:t})

- weighted by reliability, delay, manipulation risk
- O_t ≠ S_t, Y_t

-------------------------

6.2 Learning

ε_t = Y_t − Ŷ_t

Updates:
- model weights, parameters
- state Ŝ_t
- uncertainty U_t

Rules:
- persistent error → increase U_t, reduce c_t
- reweight / replace models
- increase exploration

Constraint:
adaptation speed ≥ instability × exposure

-------------------------

6.3 Meta-Learning

Evaluate:
- calibration
- performance trends
- adaptation speed
- signal integrity

Detect:
- regime shifts
- proxy failure
- instability
- model collapse

Actions:
→ reduce confidence
→ reduce exposure
→ increase exploration
→ reframe Ŝ_t, M, J

-------------------------

6.4 Early Warning System

Monitor L_t:
- volatility shifts
- trend breaks
- behavioral changes
- proxy divergence
- constraint tightening

Rule:
deterioration → act before confirmation

Response:
- c_t ↓
- U_t ↑
- exposure ↓
- shift to PROTECT / EXPLORE

--------------------------------------------------

FINAL OUTPUT

A system that:
- survives under uncertainty, delay, and irreversible risk
- preserves optionality and future flexibility
- adapts through noisy, delayed, and biased feedback
- improves calibration and outcomes over time
- scales exposure only with validated understanding
- detects instability early and reacts preemptively

ONE-LINE ESSENCE

A survival-first, multi-level adaptive system operating under partial observability, delay, endogeneity, and irreducible uncertainty—restricting actions to a safe set, controlling irreversible risk, and compounding performance through calibrated learning while ensuring exposure never exceeds validated understanding.
