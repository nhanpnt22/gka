3. VALUE & OBJECTIVE (HIERARCHICAL, SURVIVAL-CONSTRAINED — FINAL)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Objective:
Select action a_t to maximize long-term system viability and performance:
max_a J(a | Ŝ_t, ĉ_t)

--------------------------------------------------

Hierarchical Constraints (Strict Priority):

1. Survival (Hard Constraint):
P(irreversible loss within horizon H | Ŝ_t, ĉ_t, a) < δ

Definition:
- irreversible loss = state where recovery to viable operation is infeasible under constraints ĉ_t
- includes delay-induced irreversibility:
 if Δt_feedback > Δt_recovery → treat action as irreversible

--------------------------------------------------

2. Constraint Respect (Hard Constraint, Non-Terminal):
P(non-recoverable constraint violation | Ŝ_t, ĉ_t, a) < ε_c

Where:
- constraints defined by ĉ_t (risk, liquidity, irreversibility, delay, resources)

--------------------------------------------------

Feasible Set:

A_safe = {a : satisfies (1) and (2)}

All subsequent objectives are evaluated only over A_safe.

--------------------------------------------------

Second-Order Objectives (Within Feasible Set):

3. Optionality Preservation:
maximize Ω(a)

Where:
Ω(a) = E[ |A_{t+1}| | S_{t+1} ~ P(· | Ŝ_t, a), ĉ_{t+1} ]

--------------------------------------------------

4. Learning (Information Gain):
maximize IG(a)

Where:
IG(a) = E[ U_epistemic,t − U_epistemic,t+1 | a ]

--------------------------------------------------

5. Risk-Adjusted Optimization:
maximize E_{P(Y | Ŝ_t, a)}[Y] − λ · Risk(a)

--------------------------------------------------

Full Objective (Operational Form):

max_{a ∈ A_safe} E_{P(Y | Ŝ_t, a)}[Y]

with preference ordering (within A_safe):
- higher Ω(a)
- higher IG(a)
- lower Risk(a)

--------------------------------------------------

Uncertainty Integration:

All evaluations conditioned on:
P(Y | Ŝ_t, a), U_t

Implications:
- high U_t → shrink effective differentiation between actions
- high U_t → penalize aggressive or irreversible actions

--------------------------------------------------

Confidence Adjustment (Ranking Only):

J_eff(a) = c_t · E[Y | Ŝ_t, a] − (1 − c_t) · DownsideRisk(a)

Where:
c_t = f(U_t, ε_cal,t, disagreement, N_eff,t)

Role:
- used only to rank actions within A_safe
- cannot override feasibility or hierarchy

--------------------------------------------------

Endogeneity Awareness:

a_t → (S_{t+k}, C_{t+k}) → A_safe,t+k

--------------------------------------------------

Robustness Requirement:

min_{P ∈ U(Ŝ_t, U_t)} E_P[Y | a] ≥ τ

Constraint:
- robustness cannot admit actions that violate (1) or (2)
- τ reflects minimum acceptable performance under worst-case plausible model

--------------------------------------------------

Output:

- feasible action set: A_safe
- ranked actions within set: J(a), J_eff(a)

--------------------------------------------------

One-Line Essence:

A survival-first objective system that restricts actions to a probabilistically safe set under uncertain and evolving constraints, then optimizes value, optionality, and learning within that set—while ranking actions by confidence-adjusted outcomes and enforcing robustness under model and state uncertainty.
