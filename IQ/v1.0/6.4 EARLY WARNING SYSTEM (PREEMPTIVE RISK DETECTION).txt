6.4 EARLY WARNING SYSTEM (PREEMPTIVE RISK DETECTION)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Function:
- detects early signals of regime change, instability, or hidden risk
- enables action BEFORE irreversible loss or constraint violation occurs
- compensates for delayed and distorted feedback from Level 0

--------------------------------------------------

Inputs:

- leading indicators: L_t
- model diagnostics: ε_t, ε_cal,t, D_t, N_eff,t
- state signals: Ŝ_t, ĉ_t
- uncertainty: U_t
- observation stream: Õ_t

--------------------------------------------------

Leading Indicators (L_t):

Track deviations and structural signals in:

1. Volatility / Variance Shifts:
- sudden increase in dispersion or tail risk

2. Trend / Regime Breaks:
- breakdown of persistent patterns or correlations

3. Behavioral Changes:
- shifts in agent behavior (adversarial adaptation, crowding, feedback loops)

4. Proxy Divergence:
- decoupling between proxies (Ŝ_t components) and realized outcomes Y_t

5. Liquidity / Constraint Signals:
- tightening capacity, slippage, or constraint proximity (ĉ_t ↑)

--------------------------------------------------

Signal Properties:

- leading (precede realized outcomes Y_t)
- noisy and probabilistic (false positives possible)
- partially observed and model-dependent
- may conflict across indicators

Implication:
- signals increase uncertainty, not certainty

--------------------------------------------------

Detection Logic:

If ANY of the following persist or co-occur:

- L_t shows structural deviation from baseline
- ε_t ↑ or ε_cal,t ↑ (early error drift)
- model disagreement D_t ↑
- effective diversity N_eff,t ↓
- proxy instability or inconsistency in Ŝ_t

Then:
→ flag potential regime shift / instability

--------------------------------------------------

Response Rule (Preemptive):

Upon deterioration signal:

1. Reduce confidence:
c_t ↓

2. Increase uncertainty:
U_t ↑

3. Adjust decision mode (Level 4):
→ shift toward EXPLORE or PROTECT

4. Reduce exposure:
- shrink position sizes
- tighten risk limits

5. Increase exploration:
- prioritize IG(a)
- test alternative models / hypotheses

6. Trigger structural review (Level 6.3):
- reassess Ŝ_t, M, and assumptions

--------------------------------------------------

Timing Principle (Critical):

Act BEFORE confirmation:

- do not wait for realized losses Y_t
- treat early warnings as asymmetric signals:
 downside risk of inaction > cost of precaution

--------------------------------------------------

Failure Modes:

- ignoring early signals (late reaction)
- overreacting to noise (false positives)
- relying on lagging indicators only
- suppressing signals due to recent success

--------------------------------------------------

Output:

- warning state (normal / caution / critical)
- updated confidence c_t and uncertainty U_t
- preemptive adjustments:
 • exposure reduction
 • mode shift (EXPLORE / PROTECT)
 • increased monitoring and exploration

--------------------------------------------------

Interface to System:

- feeds into:
 • Level 4 (decision mode switching)
 • Level 6.2 (learning via error anticipation)
 • Level 6.3 (meta-learning and structural adaptation)

--------------------------------------------------

One-Line Essence:

A preemptive detection layer that monitors leading, imperfect signals of instability and regime change—triggering early reductions in confidence, exposure, and model trust to act before delayed feedback reveals irreversible damage.
