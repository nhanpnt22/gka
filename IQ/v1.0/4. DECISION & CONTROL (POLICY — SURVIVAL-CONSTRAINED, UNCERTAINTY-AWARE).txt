4. DECISION & CONTROL (POLICY — SURVIVAL-CONSTRAINED, UNCERTAINTY-AWARE)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Function:
- selects actions under uncertainty, constraints, and model limitations
- maps state, predictions, and objectives into executable decisions

--------------------------------------------------

Inputs:

- state: Ŝ_t, ĉ_t
- predictive distribution: P(Y | Ŝ_t, a)
- uncertainty: U_t
- confidence: c_t
- diagnostics: ε_t, ε_cal,t, disagreement D_t, N_eff,t
- feasible set: A_safe (from Level 3)

--------------------------------------------------

Decision Structure:

a_t ∈ A_safe

--------------------------------------------------

Regime Classification (Decision Modes):

1. PROTECT (Capital Preservation Mode)

Trigger if ANY of:
- high irreversible risk (near survival boundary)
- high uncertainty: U_t ↑
- constraint proximity: ĉ_t near binding
- instability / regime shift signals
- low confidence: c_t ↓

Action:
- minimize exposure
- prioritize reversibility and liquidity
- avoid tail-risk amplification

--------------------------------------------------

2. EXPLORE (Information Acquisition Mode)

Trigger if:
- model disagreement: D_t ↑
- rising prediction error: ε_t ↑
- calibration drift: ε_cal,t ↑
- moderate uncertainty with bounded downside risk

Action:
- prioritize actions with high IG(a) within A_safe
- take small, diversified, low-risk positions

Constraint:
- exploration remains strictly within A_safe

--------------------------------------------------

3. EXPLOIT (Controlled Optimization Mode)

Trigger if ALL of:
- stable regime
- low uncertainty: U_t ↓
- high confidence: c_t ↑
- low disagreement: D_t ↓
- constraints non-binding

Action:
- allocate to highest-ranked actions
- optimize risk-adjusted expected value

Constraint:
- size scaled by confidence and robustness

--------------------------------------------------

Survival Gate (Pre-Decision Filter):

All candidate actions must pass:

1. Irreversibility Check:
- reject actions with material probability of irreversible loss

2. Delay Risk Check:
- if Δt_feedback > Δt_recovery → treat as irreversible

3. Constraint Violation Check:
- reject actions violating ĉ_t with high probability

4. Hidden Risk Check:
- penalize actions with significant latent exposure

5. Correlation / Systemic Risk Check:
- account for dependency across positions

6. Endogeneity Check:
- reject actions likely to degrade future state or constraints

--------------------------------------------------

Action Selection (Within A_safe):

Step 1: Feasibility
- restrict to A_safe

Step 2: Hierarchical Filtering (Level 3 Consistency)
- eliminate actions violating:
 • survival
 • constraint safety

- among remaining, filter by:
 • Ω(a) (optionality)
 • IG(a) (learning)
 • Risk(a)

Step 3: Ranking

J_eff(a) = c_t · E[Y | Ŝ_t, a] − (1 − c_t) · DownsideRisk(a)

Step 4: Selection

a_t = argmax over remaining set

--------------------------------------------------

Action Sizing Rule:

size(a) ∝ c_t × Evidence(a) × Stability_t

Where:
- Evidence(a): strength and consistency of signal
- Stability_t: inverse of regime instability

Constraints:
- U_t ↑ → size ↓
- tail risk ↑ → size ↓
- constraint proximity ↑ → size ↓

--------------------------------------------------

Portfolio / Exposure Constraints:

- total exposure ≤ risk budget
- marginal exposure respects constraint buffers
- concentration limits enforced (correlation-aware)
- d(exposure)/dt ≤ learning rate

Implication:
- exposure cannot grow faster than system understanding

--------------------------------------------------

Dynamic Adjustment:

If diagnostics deteriorate:
- ε_t ↑ or ε_cal,t ↑ or D_t ↑

Then:
→ reduce exposure
→ shift toward EXPLORE or PROTECT modes

--------------------------------------------------

Output:

- selected action: a_t ∈ A_safe
- action size: size(a_t)
- updated exposure profile

--------------------------------------------------

One-Line Essence:

A regime-switching, survival-constrained decision policy that filters actions through strict safety gates, enforces hierarchical objectives, and applies confidence-adjusted ranking—scaling exposure with uncertainty while limiting irreversible risk and adapting to instability, error, and model disagreement.
