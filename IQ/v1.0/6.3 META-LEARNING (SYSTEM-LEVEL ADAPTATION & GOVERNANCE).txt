6.3 META-LEARNING (SYSTEM-LEVEL ADAPTATION & GOVERNANCE)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Function:
- evaluates performance of the entire system across time
- detects structural breakdowns beyond local model or state errors
- governs global adjustments to state representation, models, objectives, and policy

--------------------------------------------------

Inputs:

- prediction diagnostics: ε_t, ε_cal,t
- uncertainty: U_t
- model metrics: D_t (disagreement), N_eff,t (effective diversity)
- performance history: realized Y_{1:t}, drawdowns, recovery speed
- state estimates: Ŝ_t
- weighted observations: Õ_t
- execution diagnostics (Level 5)

--------------------------------------------------

Evaluation Dimensions:

1. Calibration:
- alignment between predicted and realized distributions
- stability of ε_cal,t over time

2. Performance Trends:
- cumulative outcomes vs expectations
- drawdowns, volatility, and recovery dynamics

3. Adaptation Speed:
- responsiveness to changing conditions
- lag between regime change and model adjustment

4. Signal Integrity:
- reliability of Õ_t
- proxy stability (feature–outcome relationships)

--------------------------------------------------

Detection Layer:

Identify structural issues beyond local noise:

1. Regime Shift:
- persistent ε_t
- rising U_t and instability
- breakdown in model calibration

2. Proxy Failure:
- divergence between Õ_t and realized outcomes
- loss of predictive power in state variables

3. Structural Instability:
- increased volatility, correlation, or constraint activation
- shrinking feasible action space

4. Model Degeneracy:
- N_eff,t ↓ (loss of diversity)
- convergence to correlated failure modes

--------------------------------------------------

Decision Rules (System-Level):

If structural deterioration detected:

→ reduce confidence c_t globally  
→ reduce total exposure  
→ increase exploration rate  
→ tighten risk constraints  

If severe or persistent:

→ reframe state representation Ŝ_t (Level 1)  
→ expand / replace model set M (Level 2)  
→ adjust objective priorities J(a) (Level 3)  
→ enforce PROTECT mode (Level 4)

--------------------------------------------------

Adaptation Constraints:

- avoid overreaction to short-term noise
- require persistence for structural changes
- adaptation speed ≥ environment change × exposure

If violated:
→ force exposure reduction

--------------------------------------------------

Global Consistency Check:

Ensure alignment across levels:

- state (Ŝ_t) remains predictive
- models (M) remain calibrated
- objectives (J) remain feasible under constraints
- decisions (a_t) remain survival-consistent

--------------------------------------------------

Failure Modes:

- delayed recognition of regime shift
- overfitting meta-signals (false positives)
- failure to escalate from local to global correction
- persistence in invalid state/model structure

--------------------------------------------------

Output:

- system-level corrections:
  • updated confidence c_t
  • adjusted exposure limits
  • revised exploration/exploitation balance
  • structural updates to Ŝ_t, M, J

--------------------------------------------------

Interface to System:

- feeds back into:
  • Level 1 (state redesign)
  • Level 2 (model restructuring)
  • Level 3 (objective adjustment)
  • Level 4 (policy override)

--------------------------------------------------

One-Line Essence:

Meta-learning monitors the entire system for structural failure—detecting regime shifts, proxy breakdowns, and instability—and enforces global corrections to maintain survival, calibration, and alignment with reality.
