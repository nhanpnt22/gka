COGNITION–REALITY LEVELS (Optimized v2.2)
=========================================

0. Generative Reality
Function:
- underlying process generating all states and change

Output:
- conditions and constraints of reality

--------------------------------------------------

1. Ontology (State Space)
Function:
- defines what exists and the minimal sufficient variables required for decision-making

Output:
- structured state representation

--------------------------------------------------

2. Epistemology (Modeling & Prediction)
Function:
- builds representations of reality (models)
- generates explicit, probabilistic predictions

Includes:
- beliefs
- hypotheses
- competing models
- uncertainty representation

Output:
- predictive models
- testable expectations (with probabilities)

--------------------------------------------------

3. Value & Objective (Selection)
Function:
- defines goals and what matters
- selects outcomes to optimize

Output:
- objective functions aligned with real-world outcomes

--------------------------------------------------

4. Decision & Control (Policy)
Function:
- selects actions under:
  - uncertainty
  - constraints
  - time horizons

Includes:
- protect (limit downside)
- explore (reduce uncertainty)
- exploit (maximize expected value)
- irreversibility handling
- action sizing (based on confidence, evidence, risk)

Output:
- chosen actions

--------------------------------------------------

5. Execution & Interaction
Function:
- executes actions in real environments
- interacts with systems and agents (including adversarial actors)

Output:
- real-world effects and consequences

--------------------------------------------------

6. Feedback & Adaptation (Learning Loop)
Function:
- converts real-world outcomes into system improvement

--------------------------------------------------

6.1 Observation (Reality Capture)
- record actual outcomes
- validate proxy vs real outcomes
- assign confidence weights:
  - source reliability
  - manipulation risk

Output:
- weighted observations

--------------------------------------------------

6.2 Learning (Error Correction)
- compute prediction vs outcome error
- update:
  - models
  - probabilities
  - strategies
  - problem framing

Rule:
- persistent error → model replacement + increased exploration

Output:
- updated system components

--------------------------------------------------

6.3 Meta-Learning (System Regulation)
- evaluate over time:
  - calibration (confidence vs reality)
  - model performance trends
  - signal quality
  - adaptation speed

- detect:
  - regime shifts
  - metric failure
  - structural breakdown

Actions:
→ reduce confidence  
→ increase exploration  
→ reassess assumptions  

Output:
- system-level adjustments

--------------------------------------------------

Final Output:
- improved system aligned with reality over time

--------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM DYNAMICS
---------------

Forward Flow:
0 → 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6

Core Loop Mapping:
State (1) → Model & Predict (2) → Decide (4) → Act (5) → Observe & Update (6)

Recursive Adaptation:
6 → 1 (state redefined)  
6 → 2 (models updated)  
6 → 3 (objectives refined)  

Closed System:
continuous, recursive alignment with reality over time

--------------------------------------------------

ONE-LINE ESSENCE
----------------

A multi-level system that transforms reality into decisions and continuously corrects itself through prediction, action, feedback, and adaptation.
